Project 0: Camera & Perspective

By Ishir Garg

Part 1: Selfie — The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Take a picture of your friend (or yourself) from close up. You get a typical distorted selfie image. Now step back several feet from your subject, zoom in, and take a second picture. Try to get the face in the second photo to be the same size as in the first photo. If you've done things right, the second portrait should look much better than the first one. Think about why this is.

Observations

The closed-up selfie compresses my face horizontally, whereas the farther away but zoomed in selfie looks more natural and realistic.

Analysis

This happens because when the camera lens is close to my face, some parts of my face are disproportionately closer to the lens. As a result, some facial features seem larger than others. When the camera is farther away, but zoomed, these differences in distance are geometrically less, and are not exaggerated.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Observations

The zoomed in image looks much flatter and more compressed than the image taken without zoom.

Analysis

This is a similar effect to the previous part. When we stand farther away, near and far away objects subtend a similar angle in the field of view, so they look more similar in size and distance. When we go closer and don't zoom in however, there are more drastic differences in the angle subtended by nearby versus far objects, exaggerating their differences and creating more depth.
